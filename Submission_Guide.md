# ElevenHacks Submission Guide

Welcome to ElevenHacks! This guide is your path to submitting a winning project—one that not only impresses judges but also stands out in the gallery and on social media.

---

## 📈 What Makes a Top Submission?

**Great demos solve a real problem _and_ are fun to watch.** The difference between a forgettable entry and a prize-winner is often deciding _how_ you approach your build and _how_ you present it.

**Pro Tip:**  
Most participants focus on their code and rush the demo video at the last minute. _Flip that._ Plan for your video to take at least half of your time—it’s the door to your project.

## 1. What to Build

Build something **original, useful, and memorable**. Before crafting your video or social posts, focus here.

- **Push the limits** of ElevenLabs and that week's sponsor technology. Don’t just use basic API features—explore advanced ones and combine tools in creative ways.
- **Go beyond tutorials**. Avoid the “getting started” sample. Dig deep, surface hidden gems in the docs, and create genuinely new features.
- **Chain tools together**. The best projects merge multiple APIs in unexpected, impressive ways.
- **Solve genuine problems**. Demos should be something real people want—not just fun toys for the hackathon.

### What Makes a Submission Go Viral?

- **Solve a pain point:** “Wow, I need this!”
- **Show, don’t tell:** Demonstrate outcomes in the real world.
- **Surprise your audience:** Build for the jaw-drop or “I didn’t know that was possible” moment.
- **Leverage the moment:** Tie your idea to trending topics, memes, or current events for extra impact.

## 2. How to Make a Killer Video

Your video _is_ your submission’s pitch. Judges will watch it before reading a line of your code.

### The 5-Second Rule

- You get **5 seconds** to hook viewers.
- Communicate the value _immediately_—not tech details, not a greeting.

#### Example Hooks

- “This AI turns any podcast into an animated short.”
- “This app reads your codebase and writes the docs.”
- “Watch me clone any voice in real time with one click.”

### Crafting Your Hook

Your video’s hook (the first 3–5 seconds) should:
- **Lead with the outcome:** Show the finished product, then reverse-engineer how it works.
- **Make a bold claim or demo:** E.g. “I replaced my entire support team with one API call.”
- **Start with ‘wow’:** Open with the most visually impressive or dramatic moment.

### Recording Tips

- **Keep it concise:** 60–90 seconds is ideal. Ruthlessly cut fluff.
- **Script the flow, not every word:** 3–5 bullet points to keep your delivery natural.
- **Show, don’t tell:** 80% of the video should demo the product, not slides or talking heads.
- **Film in the real world:** Use your project in actual scenarios for maximum relatability and shareability.
- **Highlight ElevenLabs and the sponsor:** Make clear and visual references to the required tech & API usage.
- **Prioritize audio:** Use a good mic, quiet space, or ElevenLabs voice for narration.
- **Record in 1080p or higher** for clarity after compressing/uploading.

### Setting the Mood with Music

- Background music transforms a screen recording into a story.
- _Upbeat = energetic. Minimal = elegant._ Pick music that matches your project’s vibe.
- **Generate your track with ElevenLabs Music:**  
  _E.g._ “upbeat tech demo, synth, 90 bpm” for seamless integration with your demo.
- **Keep music subtle** (15–20% volume), supporting your narration.

## 3. How to Submit

### Writing Your Project Description
Keep submissions concise. Cover:
1. **What you built:** One sentence—what is it, what problem does it solve?
2. **How you used the tech:** Explicitly mention ElevenLabs and the partner; note APIs/features/workflows used.
3. **What makes it special:** Highlight your creative twist, unexpected result, or unique impact.

_Avoid copying your video script—this is your chance to add brief context for judges to skim._

### Submitting a Cover Image
- Your cover image is your submission’s face in the gallery.
- **Best options:**
  - Clean in-app screenshot
  - Graphic or video frame capturing the “wow” moment
- **Avoid:** Stock images and generic placeholders.
- **Think in thumbnails:** Design for small size—bold visuals, short text, easily readable.

---

### Maximize Points by Posting Everywhere

Earn **+50 points per platform** (X, LinkedIn, Instagram, TikTok). Post on all four for +200 points!

**Platform-specific tips:**

- **X (Twitter):** Lead with your one-liner. Post video natively. Tag [@elevenlabsio](https://twitter.com/elevenlabsio), the partner, and use #ElevenHacks.
- **LinkedIn:** Story format works: “Built X for #ElevenHacks, here’s what I learned.” Add native video, keep under 200 words.
- **Instagram Reels & TikTok:** Crop for 9:16 vertical format. Add captions (most watch on mute). Place your best moment at the start.

## 4. Submission Checklist

- [ ] Spent half your time on the video, not just code
- [ ] Value prop appears in the first 5 seconds
- [ ] Video under 90 seconds (60s even better)
- [ ] Captions added for mute viewers
- [ ] Engaging background music
- [ ] Posted to as many social platforms as possible (+50 pts each!)
- [ ] Clear, concise description in the submission form
- [ ] Repo link and/or live demo included
- [ ] Tagged @elevenlabs & the sponsor brand
